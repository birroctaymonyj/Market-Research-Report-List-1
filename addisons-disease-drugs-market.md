<p><p>The Addison’s Disease drugs market comprises two primary types: oral and parenteral drugs. Oral drugs are typically corticosteroids, such as hydrocortisone or prednisone, taken daily to manage hormone deficiencies. They are preferred for their convenience and ease of administration. Parenteral drugs, administered via injection or infusion, are used in cases requiring rapid hormone replacement or when oral intake is not feasible. Both drug types are essential for maintaining hormonal balance and preventing adrenal crises in affected patients.</p></p>
